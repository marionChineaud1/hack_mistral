# 2. The specialised investigator

**Time:** 30 seconds

## Slide content

### One specialised workflow, six investigative responsibilities

| Responsibility | Question it answers |
| --- | --- |
| Planner | What claims can actually be checked? |
| Source Hunter | Where did the post originate and what is the chronology? |
| Institutional Evidence | What do authoritative sources say? |
| Context Analyst | What dates, scope, exceptions, or baselines are missing? |
| Rhetorical Analyst | How do wording, certainty, emotion, and omissions frame the message? |
| Evidence Judge | What is supported, contradicted, or still unknown? |

## Speaker notes

“North is a specialised investigator for viral information. It does not jump to a verdict. It first turns the post into verifiable claims, traces the origin, looks for institutional evidence, and checks the missing context.

It then analyses rhetoric separately: wording, certainty, emotional framing, and omissions. That is an analysis of the content, not a judgement about the author’s intention.

Finally, it reconciles supporting and contradictory evidence, names its limitations, and states what could change the conclusion.”

## Important clarification

“These are visible specialised responsibilities inside one orchestrated workflow, not a claim that we operate six independent services. Each responsibility has a clear investigative question and a visible contribution to the final report.”

## Transition

“This workflow is guided by a Skill, but North is deliberately more than a Skill.”
