# 8. Jury pitch cheat sheet

Use these prompts to speak naturally, rather than memorising the full pitch.

## The story in 11 points

1. **Problem:** Information moves at feed speed. Verification does not.
2. **Product:** Vera is the investigation layer for online information.
3. **User value:** Paste one link, then see the evidence, context, uncertainty, and sources behind it.
4. **Accessible:** Mistral Vibe makes Vera usable by anyone, without technical setup, tool configuration, or prompt-engineering expertise.
5. **Workflow:** Vera traces the origin, checks live and institutional sources, recovers missing context, and analyses framing.
6. **Trust:** It does not give a black-box answer. It shows what supports, challenges, or could change the conclusion.
7. **Technology:** The Skill orchestrates, MCP tools retrieve live evidence, structured persistence saves the investigation, and Nuxt renders the report.
8. **MVP:** X posts in, durable investigations out.
9. **Future inputs:** Web pages, articles, PDFs, images, and video.
10. **Future capabilities:** Provenance and metadata checks, transcription, authenticity signals, alerts, source snapshots, and collaborative follow-up.
11. **Closing:** Before information earns attention, it should earn investigation.

## The shortest possible version

> “Vera makes responsible verification as easy as sharing a link. In Mistral Vibe, anyone can launch a live investigation with no technical expertise. Vera retrieves evidence, explains uncertainty, and saves a shareable report. We start with X posts, then expand the same investigation pipeline to every format where information spreads.”
