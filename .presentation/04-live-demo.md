# 4. Live demo

**Time:** Start at 0:00; reveal results at 1:05–1:45

## Demo sequence

1. **Start with a supported X post URL in Mistral Vibe.**
   - Do this before the first slide and say: “While Vera investigates this post live, we will show you what happens behind the scenes.”
   - Vera extracts the post and normalises the canonical source.

2. **Present the problem, solution, agent, and architecture while the live investigation runs.**
   - Use sections 1 to 3 as the investigation progresses.

3. **Show the live investigation statuses when they appear.**
   - Recherche de la publication d’origine et de la chronologie
   - Vérification auprès de sources institutionnelles
   - Comparaison des sources indépendantes
   - Recherche du contexte manquant
   - Analyse du cadrage et des incertitudes

4. **Point out the evidence boundary.**
   - Say: “A Community Note is a research lead, not an automatic refutation. Vera still checks independent and institutional evidence.”

5. **Show persistence.**
   - Highlight: *Enregistrement de l’enquête détaillée*.
   - Say: “The complete structured investigation is saved only after synthesis, with citations linked to its sources.”

6. **Reveal the concise Vibe response.**
   - Point out the cautious conclusion, one decisive cited finding, and the **Consulter l’enquête complète** link.

7. **Open the Nuxt report.**
   - Highlight claim breakdown, evidence groups, chronology, missing context, rhetoric analysis, sources, and “what could change the conclusion”.
   - Say: “This is database-backed and reconstructable without the chat history or MCP logs.”

## What to say about uncertainty

“Vera does not pretend that every claim has a clean binary answer. When external evidence is unavailable or incomplete, it says **Preuves insuffisantes** instead of inventing certainty.”

## Failure handling, if needed

“Vera does not replace a failed live source with mock evidence. If extraction or persistence fails, it says so and does not generate a fabricated report link.”
