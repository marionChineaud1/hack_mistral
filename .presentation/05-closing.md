# 5. Closing

**Time:** 15 seconds

## Slide content

> Most AI assistants give an answer. Vera shows the work required to deserve one.

- Evidence, not authority.
- Uncertainty, not false certainty.
- A durable report, not an opaque chat response.

## Speaker notes

“What you have seen today is the MVP: X posts in, durable investigations out. But Vera is designed as an investigation layer for the internet, not as an X-only product.

Next, we will support web pages, articles, and PDFs. Then we will extend the same investigation pipeline to images and video, with reverse-origin checks, metadata analysis, transcription, and authenticity signals. We can then add source snapshots, evidence-change alerts, collaborative follow-ups, and specialised workflows for science, health, politics, and finance.

Vera does not replace journalists or professional fact-checkers. It gives people a transparent starting point: what we know, what remains uncertain, and what evidence could change the conclusion.

Before information earns attention, it should earn investigation.”

## Product vision

| --- | --- | --- |

## Likely jury questions

### Is Vera deciding the truth?

“No. Vera presents evidence quality, contradictory findings, limitations, and missing context. Its conclusion is cautious and revisable.”

### Why does rhetoric analysis matter?

“Factual accuracy and fair presentation are different questions. A claim can be partly factual while still being framed in a misleadingly certain or incomplete way.”

### Why save the report?

“Saving structured citations and uncertainty makes the result inspectable, shareable, and durable beyond a single chat session.”
